# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Personal / solo admin managing a self-hosted anime library, typically on a laptop or desktop within a home network. The user is technically capable (self-hosts, configures RSS, manages downloads) and treats Bakarr as a utility for organizing and automating their collection. Context is quiet, focused, and often multi-tasking alongside other media consumption.

## Product Purpose

Bakarr is a self-hosted anime library manager. It helps users organize their collection, track wanted releases, monitor RSS feeds, manage downloads, and browse their catalog. Success means the library stays accurate and up-to-date with minimal manual effort.

## Brand Personality

Clean, clinical, precise. Neutral confidence. The UI is a calm frame around anime artwork — it never competes for attention. Warmth comes from the content, not decoration.

Three words: **precise**, **neutral**, **confident**.

## Anti-references

- Cluttered anime fan sites with neon gradients, mascots, or excessive decoration.
- Generic SaaS dashboards with card-grid overload and blue-primary-everywhere.
- Streaming-service clones (Netflix/Disney+ carousels) — this is a library manager, not a player.
- Corporate enterprise tools with dense grey tables and 90s aesthetics.

## Design Principles

1. **Content is the star** — UI frames anime artwork; it never competes with it.
2. **Calm density** — information-rich without clutter. Every pixel serves a purpose.
3. **Neutral confidence** — tints and tones feel precise, not cold. Warmth comes from artwork.
4. **Consistency over novelty** — predictable patterns across catalog, wanted, downloads, and settings.
5. **Respect the solo admin** — optimize for single-user speed and decision-making.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Good contrast ratios, full keyboard navigation, screen reader support. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. No known specific user needs beyond standard best practices.
